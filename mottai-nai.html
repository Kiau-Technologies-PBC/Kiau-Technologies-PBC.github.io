What is Mottai-nai?

It's a phrase you might commonly hear a grandmother tell a child who hasn't finished their plate in Japan.

You may also hear it while someone is receiving a gift, expressing that the gift is unmerited or undeserved.

Today this Japanese phrase has become a global call to action to rethink our lack of awareness and consideration to the resources we consume for granted.

At Kiau Tech, it's a guiding principle that informs how we approach all of our projects, and helps us tirelessly identify and redefine waste—from physical organics to CPU resources.

We're far from the first to be guided by this phrase. Mottainai was first used as a call to action to the global stage by Wangarĩ Maathai, the legendary Kenyan social, environmental, and political activist who founded the Green Belt Movement. More recently it has since sparked UN-backed youth declarations of zero waste and circularity globally. Youth Lead the Zero Waste Movement: The Mottainai Declaration Goes Global
