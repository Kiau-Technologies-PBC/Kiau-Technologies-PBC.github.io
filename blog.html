PCB for the Mothership â€“ MPCB v1.0

Posted on January 30th, 2025 by Burt Kim

Burt Kim has initiated the design of MPCB v1.0, a custom PCB for the Mothership, integrating ESP32, an RFM9x LoRa chip, and a microSD card holder. This redesign aims to enhance affordability and efficiency compared to the current Mothership architecture, which relies on ESP32 or Arduino Uno, an RFM9x chip, and a Raspberry Pi.

The new PCB will streamline data collection and storage by leveraging LoRaWAN communication. Incoming data from the RFM9x chip will be processed by the ESP32, and instead of relying on a Raspberry Pi for storage, the processed data will be stored directly on a microSD card. Additionally, the Mothership will continue uploading data to Firebase, ensuring cloud accessibility and real-time monitoring. By consolidating components and optimizing the data pipeline, MPCB v1.0 aims to create a cost-effective and scalable solution for efficient long-range data communication.

Disassembly of Vapes

Posted on March 11, 2025 by Spencer Kirsch | Max Van Dorn

Recently, we began to collect disposable vape pens in the hopes we can utilize the components inside for more than just vaping.

There are many resources in these devices that we believe can serve another purpose. These items include the batteries, resistance heaters, the body or container of the vape, the wick and more. Almost all of the components can be reused. The goal, 95% of the devices materials will be recycled. Each part will have a unique function for our projects. The lithium-ion batteries can be reused and charged again for powering portable chargers and hand warmers. The resistance heaters will be helpful for filament joining and hand warmers. The wick or the cotton holding the e-liquid can be used for mycelium substrates. The bodies are made of either aluminum or plastic. The aluminum is good for housing or to conduct heat in hand warmers. And the plastic (composed of ABS) which can be shredded for 3D printing material. The gaskets on the vapes are good for ingress protection. Mass measurements will be taken to ensure the % we are recycling is accurate.
