What is Kiau?

Kiau, loosely translated to “Suiting trees” is a both nod to revering trees and the role they play in ecosystems across the world, as well as Japanese woodworking. Specifically, Japanese joinery does not go against the grain of the wood, and instead works in harmony with the natural grain and inherent material properties of the wood. Japanese joinery acknowledges that wood is not a dead, static material, and expands or contracts with changes in humidity and temperature. By using interlocking joints that rely on friction and compression instead of glue or nails, structures built with joinery can shift without losing integrity.

So?

Similarly, Kiau Technologies PBC is a project committed imagining technology designed in harmony with the natural environment. Our mission is to:

  • 1. Radically rethink our everyday interactions with technology
  • 2. Challenge the notion/dichotomy that man and nature are separate
  • 3. Designs tools and tech as living companions instead of tools of surveillance and extraction

Our team's collaboration and technical expertise have enabled us to overcome various challenges, ensuring continuous progress towards commercializing our innovative solutions. The dedication and passion of key team members have been instrumental in driving the project forward, as we continue to expand our knowledge and skills to meet the project's needs.
